Jurisdiction & Regulatory Notices

Meridian Capital Holdings Limited  ·  Last updated: 16 July 2026

This page sets out market-specific notices for clients based in South Africa, the United Kingdom, and the European Union. It supplements, and should be read alongside, our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

General

Meridian Capital Holdings Limited is registered in Hong Kong and acts as a commercial representative and advisory intermediary. Meridian is not a law firm, is not a licensed customs broker or agent in any jurisdiction, and does not provide regulated legal, tax, or customs advice. Nothing on this website or in our service delivery should be construed as such advice.

South Africa

Meridian does not act as your customs clearing agent. Under South African customs legislation, clearing agents must be licensed and located in South Africa, and a foreign principal must appoint a locally registered agent for customs purposes. We can help identify a licensed SA customs agent; engaging one remains your responsibility. Meridian's founder is not an admitted attorney, advocate, or conveyancer in South Africa, and nothing prepared by Meridian constitutes legal services as defined under South African law.

United Kingdom

Meridian does not act as your customs agent for UK import declarations. Under UK customs regulations, a person acting as a customs agent must generally be established in the United Kingdom. We can help identify a UK-established customs agent; engaging one remains your responsibility. Meridian's founder is not a solicitor, barrister, or otherwise authorised to carry out reserved legal activities under the Legal Services Act 2007, and general commercial documentation prepared by Meridian is not legal advice.

European Union

Meridian does not act as your customs representative for EU import declarations. Under the Union Customs Code, customs representation generally requires establishment within the EU. We can help identify an appropriately established EU customs representative; engaging one remains your responsibility. Rules on the provision of legal services vary by EU member state; where you require legal advice, we recommend engaging counsel qualified in your relevant member state.

Updates

This page may be updated as regulatory requirements in these jurisdictions change. Last updated: 16 July 2026.
