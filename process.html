PROCESS

Process

Every Meridian engagement follows a structured process. Whether the requirement is a single standalone service or a full-scope mandate from supplier search through to shipment, the steps below apply. No work commences without a signed engagement letter.

01

Initial consultation

A structured one-hour session to confirm your objective, assess sourcing feasibility, and agree scope. Fee: USD 300 / HKD 2,340 — deducted from commission if you proceed to a full engagement.

02

Engagement letter

A formal written agreement signed by both parties before any work begins. Sets out scope, commission rate, minimum fee, third-party cost treatment, and termination terms. No engagement proceeds without a signed letter.

03

Supplier identification and verification

Research, NECIPS verification, and scoring using the Meridian Supplier Assessment Protocol. Delivered as a written Supplier Shortlist Report with a primary recommendation.

04

RFQ management and negotiation

Structured RFQ drafted and distributed to the shortlisted suppliers. All quotes compared on a like-for-like basis. Recommendation and negotiation record delivered in writing.

05

Contract drafting and review

The underlying supply agreement is drafted or reviewed once commercial terms are agreed — covering price, quality standards, delivery, and remedies for breach.

06

Trade compliance and documentation

HS classification, duty advisory, certification requirements, and commercial document preparation or review — covering your destination market.

07

Quality inspection coordination

Inspection brief prepared and submitted to an accredited inspection company. Findings reviewed and an advisory note issued within 24 hours of the report.

08

Invoicing and close-out

Commission invoiced against the confirmed FOB supplier invoice value at the agreed milestone. Third-party costs invoiced separately at cost with supporting documentation attached.

Ready to begin a sourcing engagement?

Describe what you are sourcing and your target parameters. We will respond within one business day and, where appropriate, propose a structured consultation as the next step.
