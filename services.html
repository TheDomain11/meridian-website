SERVICES

Services

Meridian offers six services, available on a standalone fee basis or as part of a full engagement on commission. Every engagement begins with a signed engagement letter, and no work commences without one.

SERVICE 01

Supplier identification and verification

What we do

Meridian conducts research into manufacturers and trading companies across Alibaba, Made-in-China, Global Sources, and direct factory networks. Every shortlisted supplier is verified through China's National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System (NECIPS), confirming business registration, registered scope, export licence status, and credential consistency. Where required, Meridian conducts in-person factory visits in the Pearl River Delta region.

Deliverables

  • Meridian Supplier Longlist — all identified candidates, with source and suitability notes
  • Meridian Supplier Shortlist Report — verified candidates, scored using the Meridian Supplier Assessment Protocol, with a primary recommendation and stated reasoning
  • Meridian Factory Visit Report — where a visit is conducted, structured observations, photographic evidence, and assessment
Scope boundary: Formal third-party audits (SA8000, ISO, BSCI) fall outside Meridian's scope. These are coordinated with SGS, Bureau Veritas, or Intertek, and invoiced to the client at cost.
Meridian IP: Meridian Supplier Assessment Protocol — a proprietary five-category scoring methodology. Supplier Longlist, Shortlist Report, and Factory Visit Report templates are copyright protected.

SERVICE 02

RFQ management and negotiation

What we do

Meridian prepares a structured RFQ based on the client's complete product specification, distributes it to the verified supplier shortlist, and manages all responses and clarifications. Negotiation covers unit price, minimum order quantity, payment terms, lead time, and sample arrangements. Every quote is compared on a like-for-like basis — the same Incoterm, the same MOQ — before a recommendation is issued.

Deliverables

  • Meridian RFQ Document — formally drafted and reviewed before distribution, stating commercial terms (Incoterm basis, payment terms, liability allocation) explicitly
  • Meridian Quote Comparison Report — a tabular comparison, negotiation summary, and a written recommendation with reasoning tied to the specific terms quoted
Meridian IP: Meridian RFQ Template and Quote Comparison Template are copyright protected.

SERVICE 03

Contract drafting and review

What we do

Once commercial terms are agreed, Meridian drafts or reviews the underlying supply or purchase agreement between client and supplier — the instrument governing the transaction, distinct from the ancillary shipment documentation. This encompasses price, quantity, quality standards, delivery obligations, inspection and acceptance terms, and remedies for breach or non-conformance.

Deliverables

  • Meridian Contract Drafting Note — a drafted or reviewed supply agreement, with each clause's effect and risk allocation set out in plain terms
  • A written summary of recommended amendments, with supporting rationale, prior to execution
Scope boundary: This is contract drafting and review support provided by an unregulated agency, and does not constitute SRA-regulated legal advice. For high-value or high-risk contracts, or where a dispute is anticipated, Meridian recommends engaging independent qualified legal counsel in the relevant jurisdiction.
Meridian IP: Meridian Supply Contract Template is copyright protected, drafted with LLB/LLM legal precision.

SERVICE 04

Trade compliance and documentation

What we do

Meridian classifies each product to the correct HS tariff code, calculates applicable import duty rates, identifies applicable trade agreement preferential rates, and determines mandatory product certification requirements — covering South Africa (SARS), the United Kingdom (HMRC), and the European Union customs union. Meridian also prepares and reviews the full commercial document set for each shipment.

Deliverables

  • Meridian Trade Compliance Advisory Note — HS code with classification rationale citing the specific tariff schedule entry relied upon, duty rates, the specific trade agreement provision applied, and estimated landed duty cost
  • Certification Requirements Summary — mandatory and voluntary certifications by market, with recommended accredited laboratories
  • Commercial document set — commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin advisory, and bill of lading review note
Scope boundary: Meridian provides HS classification advisory and documentation support. Formal customs declarations and filing with SARS, HMRC, or EU customs authorities require a licensed customs broker; Meridian refers to a licensed broker and does not file on the client's behalf.
Meridian IP: Trade Compliance Advisory Note Template, Commercial Invoice Template, and Packing List Template are copyright protected. Legal precision (LLB, LLM) is applied to all advisory and disclaimer language.

SERVICE 05

Quality inspection coordination

What we do

Meridian establishes the client's quality requirements, briefs an accredited third-party inspection company (QIMA, Bureau Veritas, SGS, or Intertek), and books the pre-shipment inspection. On receipt of the report, Meridian reviews it against the client's specification and issues an advisory note within 24 hours, stating clearly whether to accept, reject, or request supplier remediation.

Deliverables

  • Meridian Inspection Brief — submitted to the inspection company in advance of the inspection
  • Meridian Inspection Advisory Note — findings summary and a clear recommendation, issued within 24 hours of the report
  • The full third-party inspection report, forwarded to the client in parallel
Scope boundary: Meridian does not conduct physical inspections. The inspection is performed by the accredited third-party company. Third-party inspection costs (approximately USD 300–400 per inspection) are invoiced at cost.
Meridian IP: Meridian Inspection Brief Template is copyright protected.

SERVICE 06

Sourcing consultation

What we do

A structured one-hour advisory session for businesses evaluating a China sourcing mandate, or seeking an independent assessment before committing to a supplier relationship. Meridian prepares and shares an agenda in advance, addresses the client's objective, product category, compliance requirements, and recommended approach, and issues a written summary note within 24 hours.

Deliverables

  • A structured one-hour consultation, with agenda prepared in advance
  • Meridian Consultation Summary Note — issued within 24 hours, recording the advice given and the recommended next steps
  • The consultation fee is credited toward commission on proceeding to a full engagement
Fee: USD 300 / HKD 2,340, a flat rate per session. On proceeding to a full engagement, credited against the commission or the USD 1,500 minimum engagement fee, whichever applies.
